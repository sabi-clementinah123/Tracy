TRADITIONAL DANCES

  • selia-lia- is played every time by Basotho teenage girls and boys
  • ndlamo -played by males and involves foot movements.
  • liphotha- Male dance which includes legs movements and make rhythms.
  • mokhibo- It is played by women while kneeling down involve movements of hands, shoulders and singing group using lechoba,sekupu and phala.
  • litolobonya- One of dance played by basotho girls and women using phala.
  • mohobelo- Basotho traditional dance played by males using machoba and sticks(molamu) and is performed in groups showing unity.
  • mangae- Dance performance made by males singing usually by initiation graduation ceremonies.
  • lipina tsa mokopu- Performed by Basotho girls.

MUSICAL INSTRUMENTS

  • lesiba- A wind instrument made from a string stretched over a stick and a feather.
  • lekolulo - Traditional flute made from reed or wood.
  • sekupu-
    • stick can,
  • Sekhankula is a musical instrument made of tin can,stick and string often used by children and herdboys ussually for entertainment.
  • thomo- A stringed bow played by women and produce soft music often often used during storytelling.
  • setolotolo- A mouth harp made of metal played by plucking while the mouth shapes the sound.
  • mamokhorong- A traditional string instrument.
  • lechoba - A stick with a warp threats sack usually used in mokhibo.
  • phala -A whistle-like instrument used to produce sounds.
  • sekupu -A drum used during traditional dances and rituals.
  • khabulubulu- instrument made from reeds or thin wood and is similar to a flute or whistle.
  • lekhitlane la lesapo-An animal bone with a tiny hole and is blown like a whistle
  • moropa-hallowed-out wood coverd with cowdhide or animal skin
  • lekhitla la lenaka-Made from cow horn and is blown in order producing echo sound
  • sekebeku sa t'sepe- A metal which is shaken to make a clagging sound.
